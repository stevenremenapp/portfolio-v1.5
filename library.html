INTRODUCTION

Before working as a software developer I worked for about 7 years in libraries throughout Metro Detroit, including large, small, academic, and public libraries. This background is what motivated me to teach myself software development. Many libraries, especially the smaller public libraries I love, rely on a hodge-podge of cheap and free-tier software to meet their always expanding public service needs. I also had the opportunity to work at Wayne State University Libraries, where I observed how effective a small software team could be when creating custom tools and resources. After years of frustration and assuming I couldn't learn software development, I finally decided to give it a try. The projects below are some of my earliest, and the fact that people actually used and enjoyed them were enough to keep me on this path. Maybe/hopefully I'll make software for libraries again.
