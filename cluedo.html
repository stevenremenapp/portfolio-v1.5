FUN | BOARD GAME RE-INVENTION

AN UNAUTHORIZED REWORKING OF CLUE

ABOUT

This game was created years ago with a couple of close friends as an attempt to insert political economy into Clue. Try it out and let me know how it goes!

RULES

Each player/potential MURRDEREEERR rolls 2 dice at the beginning of their turn. The number showing is how many √á the player/potential MURRDEREEERR has on-hand for use during their turn.

One player is designated the banker. They manage the Cluedo accounts of all players at the Cluedo National Bank (CNB) and transactions at the Open-Air Market (OAM). For performing this generous service, they start the game.

ABILITIES

Abilities can be purchased at the Open-Air Market (OAM), located in the center of the board (known to original Clue game as the "cellar" and a subsequent, less successful version as the "pool").

It costs 1 √á to enter the Open-Air Market since there are no 'doors' per se. It also costs 1 √á to exit the Open-Air Market. The player/potential MUURUDDDEEEERERERR must exit on the same side of the rectangle that they entered.

The Open-Air Market doesn't count as a room, that is, entering doesn't renew banking and guessing actions for the player/potential MUURUDDDEEEERERERR. No banking or guessing is allowed here.

Inventory of Abilities in the Open-Air Market

All abilities can be used at any time during a player's turn, including immediately upon purchase. Inventory is infinite. Following are recommended abilities and pricing, and are subject to change based on sociocultural context of the game at hand. This pricing is based on a three-person game. The writers suspect that adding more players to the game should increase the cost of abilities since they become more potent. Use reasonable judgement.
